Related Technologies

Website Creation

Many independent bloggers are not knowledgeable in website creation. Often, they use template-based website creators such as WordPress, WebFlow or Wix. These website creators are key to enabling those that aren’t skilled at web development to be able to pursue their ambitions independently, and work through drag and drop layouts that write code dynamically. One can then host a website and begin blogging.

Blogging vs Journalism

Two popular forms of online communication are blogging and journalism. It is difficult to understand what separates them. Often, they can be found in the same place. However, each form has their own tone and content. News sections written by journalists tend to be on the shorter side, with official, factual and serious sounding information. Journalists appeal to facts and collect their content in person. Also, it is much more viable to make a salary as a journalist. Bloggers appeal to emotion and make subjective, opinionated content from the comfort of their own home.

What Are Blogs?

Oxford’s definition of blog is ‘a regularly updated website or web page, typically one run by an individual or small group, that is written in an informal or conversational style.’

They are a form of mass communication, opinionated speech and a place for groups of enthusiasts can share ideas and talk. There can be blogging businesses, individual blogs, or smaller businesses, for profit, or unmonetized, however, they all share some fundamental purposes. To align with an audience, to gain a following, to generate organic traffic through their website, and potentially even to call to action. Many larger businesses use blogging to find customers, build a brand, or build authority, but blogs can be less formal; a passion project.

Some of the characteristics of blogs include:

  • Displayed in reverse chronological order. From newest to oldest posts.
  • Archives of old posts.
  • Comments where people can communicate with each other and the blog poster.
  • Side bars with social media profiles, popular content or calls to action.
  • Links to find more about the author’s story.

History and Popular Blogs

Blogs are a form of online expression and have been utilised since 1994. In the early days of blogging, one would blog as a sort of personal diary. However, they have been developed into versatile, monetizable and massive online journals and informational websites. These blogs can be run by inspired and dedicated individuals, or run by organisations, generating value by maintaining relevance to today’s society and building loyal consumers.

The Future of Blogs

The future of blogging is something hotly debated in the blogging community. Its relevance peaked from 2005-2008, however, on the surface blogging seems to be a declining mode of communication. Some of the reasons for this estimate that interest in blogging is declining is as follows:

  1. More need for quality content due to google SEO ranking and people become choosier.
  2. Alternative sources of content consumption such as vlogging.
  3. Google knowledge graph, the program that returns the information you searched for to the top of your screen, is taking organic traffic from websites.
